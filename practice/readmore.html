Religion has been a part of human civilization for as long as history can remember. From ancient tribal rituals under the stars to grand temples, mosques, churches, and monasteries, religion has shaped how people see the world and how they treat one another. But in today’s fast-changing, interconnected world, a question often comes to mind: Do we still need religion? Or has its time passed?

The roots of religion stretch back thousands of years. In ancient times, when people could not explain natural events like storms, eclipses, or illness, they often attributed them to powerful unseen forces...

At the end of the day, whether you are religious or not, what matters is how your beliefs — or lack of them — influence your actions. Do they make you more compassionate? More just? More human? That is the real question worth answering.
